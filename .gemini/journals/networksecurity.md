### [2026-05-29] Implementing NetworkSecurityInterceptEndpointGroup direct controller & LRO normalization
- **Context**: Implementing the direct controller and E2E fixtures for `NetworkSecurityInterceptEndpointGroup` (Issue #8817).
- **Problem**: 
  1. The API was not available in `cloud.google.com/go/networksecurity/apiv1`, requiring us to compile `mockgcp/apis/google/cloud/networksecurity/v1/intercept.proto` into mockgcp generated packages, and then use raw gRPC dialling in the controller.
  2. Transient millisecond-timestamped operation IDs (`operation-17800...`) generated by MockGCP caused unstable/failing E2E HTTP traffic logs comparison since they couldn't be normalized by body field extraction (since client-side interceptors are not installed on raw gRPC dials).
  3. During `go test`, we discovered an initialization panic in `interceptdeployment_identity.go` due to placeholder mismatch between `{intercept_deployment}` template parameter and the `NetworkSecurityInterceptDeploymentIdentity` struct field.
- **Solution**:
  1. Modified `mockgcp/Makefile` to compile local proto files into `mockgcp/generated/google/cloud/networksecurity/v1` and implemented `InterceptServer` mock handlers on mockgcp, dialling via `google.golang.org/api/transport/grpc` in our controller.
  2. Fixed the template mismatch in `interceptdeployment_identity.go` by renaming the placeholder to `{interceptdeployment}`.
  3. Added a robust regex normalizer (`re := regexp.MustCompile(\`operation-[0-9a-zA-Z\\-]+\`)`) to both `Normalizer.Render` and `LegacyNormalize` to dynamically replace transient gRPC LRO names with `operation-\${operationID}`.
- **Impact**: Enables stable gRPC-based direct controller testing without flaky HTTP golden log mismatches, and resolved initialization panics in networksecurity.
